1. Quick Facts & Epidemiology

  • Global burden: ~700,000 deaths/year from CRC
  • Rectal cancers: 30-40% of CRC cases
  • Rising incidence: in adults <50 years
  • Median age: 60-72 years
  • 5-year survival:
    • Stage I: 85-95%
    • Stage II: 60-80%
    • Stage III: 40-70%
    • Stage IV: 10-20%

2. Rectal Anatomy

Segments (from anal verge)

  • Lower rectum: 0-5 cm ‚Äî high APR risk
  • Mid rectum: 5-10 cm ‚Äî most common site
  • Upper rectum: 10-15 cm ‚Äî partly intraperitoneal

7. Surgery ‚Äî TME Principles

Total Mesorectal Excision (TME) = GOLD STANDARD
Sharp dissection in embryologic plane between mesorectal and pelvic fascia

Surgical Options

Procedure Indication Key Points
Low Anterior Resection (LAR) Sphincter preservation possible Coloanal anastomosis; consider diverting ileostomy
Abdominoperineal Resection (APR) Low tumor, sphincter involvement Permanent colostomy; counseling essential
TEM/TAMIS T1 favorable lesions Local excision; strict criteria (well-diff, no LVI, Sm1, <3cm)
Transanal TME (TaTME) Low tumors, selected centers Bottom-up approach; requires expertise

Red Flags for Recurrence

  • Rising CEA
  • New pelvic pain or bleeding
  • Change in bowel habits
  • Palpable mass on DRE
  • Respiratory or hepatic symptoms

9. Clinical Pearls for Rounds

Preoperative

MRI quality matters ‚Äî demand rectal protocol with CRM reporting
Tattoo tumor location endoscopically
Early stoma counseling if APR likely
Optimize nutrition; consider supplementation

Intraoperative

Aim for negative CRM ‚Äî no piecemeal excision
Diverting ileostomy for low coloanal anastomosis
Intact TME specimen ‚Äî pathologist assesses quality

Postoperative

Communicate function outcomes: LAR syndrome risk, sexual/urinary dysfunction
Review pathology at MDT ‚Äî confirm ypTNM, CRM, adjuvant plan

10. Prognostic Factors

Favorable

  • Early T/N stage
  • Negative CRM
  • No EMVI
  • Well/moderate differentiation
  • MSI-high
  • pCR after neoadjuvant therapy

Adverse

  • Positive CRM (‚â§1mm)
  • EMVI positive
  • Poor differentiation
  • High nodal burden (N2)
  • Mucinous/signet ring

TAKE-HOME MESSAGES

  1. Rectal cancer management requires MDT planning
  2. MRI is cornerstone for local staging and operative planning
  3. TME with negative CRM is essential for local control
  4. Neoadjuvant therapy is standard in T3-T4 or N+ disease
  5. Consider organ preservation only in strict protocols
  6. Molecular profiling matters for metastatic disease
  7. Surveillance detects treatable recurrences early
